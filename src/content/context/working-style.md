# Working Style

I try to operate as a **high-context, low-ego, execution-focused partner** to the team. My goal is to bring clarity, remove friction, and help the team move quickly in the right direction.

I don’t see product as the person with the answers—I see it as the role responsible for **making sure the right problems are being solved and that the team can execute effectively**.

---

## I Work Closely With Engineering

I treat engineers as partners, not ticket-takers.

In practice, that means:
- Bringing clear problem definitions, not just feature requests
- Sharing context around users, constraints, and goals
- Collaborating on tradeoffs instead of dictating solutions

I’m comfortable getting into technical details when needed, especially in systems-heavy or data-driven products. But I focus on enabling engineers to make strong decisions rather than trying to control the implementation.

The best outcomes happen when engineering has ownership—not just responsibility.

---

## I Value Clear, Direct Communication

I try to communicate in a way that is:
- **Clear** → No ambiguity about what we’re doing or why
- **Concise** → Respect people’s time
- **Honest** → Say what needs to be said, even if it’s uncomfortable

I don’t over-polish communication. I’d rather be direct and aligned than perfectly worded and unclear.

---

## I Default to Writing Things Down

If something matters, it should be written.

I use writing to:
- Clarify thinking
- Align teams
- Reduce repeated conversations
- Create a source of truth

Good documentation doesn’t mean long documentation—it means **useful, structured, and easy to reference**.

---

## I Run Focused, Purpose-Driven Meetings

I try to avoid meetings that exist just to “stay aligned.”

Every meeting should have:
- A clear purpose
- The right people (and only the right people)
- A defined outcome

If a meeting doesn’t need to exist, I’d rather handle it asynchronously.

---

## I Handle Conflict Directly

Disagreement is part of building good products.

When conflict comes up, I focus on:
- Getting to the root of the disagreement
- Aligning on goals and constraints
- Evaluating tradeoffs clearly

I don’t take disagreement personally, and I don’t avoid it. Avoiding conflict usually leads to worse decisions.

---

## I Give and Receive Feedback Honestly

I try to give feedback that is:
- Specific
- Actionable
- Grounded in outcomes

And I expect the same in return.

I don’t assume I’m right. I care more about getting to the best answer than being the one who comes up with it.

---

## I Bias Toward Action

I don’t like work that exists only in planning or discussion.

If something is unclear, I prefer to:
- Build a small version
- Test it
- Learn from it

Progress creates clarity. Waiting for clarity before moving usually slows everything down.

---

## I Care About Ownership

I value teams where people:
- Take responsibility for outcomes, not just tasks
- Follow through on commitments
- Care about the result, not just their piece of it

I try to model that myself—if something is important, I’ll stay close to it until it’s working.

---

## What “Good Work” Looks Like to Me

Good work is:
- Solving the right problem
- Delivered with a high level of quality
- Shipped in a reasonable amount of time
- Actually used and valued by real users

It’s not about perfect specs or perfect execution—it’s about **real impact**.

---

## What I Value in a Team

I do my best work on teams that:
- Communicate openly and directly
- Care about outcomes, not just output
- Are willing to challenge each other
- Move quickly and learn as they go

Low-ego, high-ownership teams consistently outperform everything else.

---

## Strong Opinion

Most teams don’t have a talent problem—they have a clarity and accountability problem.

When expectations are clear, communication is direct, and ownership is real, teams move faster and produce better work without needing more process or more people.
